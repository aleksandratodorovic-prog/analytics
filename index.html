The gap between CR and AR is where SDK/UX improvements have the most impact
Conversion Rate
65%
End-to-end: from checkout initiated to payment authorized. Includes all drop-off — UX friction, abandonment, errors, 3DS, AND declines.
Approval Rate
87%
Starts at "Pay" button click. Only measures whether the payment processor / issuing bank approved the transaction.
The Gap
35%
Users lost before payment is even attempted. This is the SDK/checkout UX territory — form friction, 3DS drop-off, slow loading, confusion.

What Affects Each Metric

Conversion Rate SDK / UX

  • SDK load time & rendering speed
  • Payment form UX & field validation
  • Payment method availability & ordering
  • 3DS challenge UX & drop-off
  • Error messages & retry flows
  • Device / browser compatibility
  • Wallet (Apple Pay / Google Pay) eligibility & presentation
  • Redirect flow completion rates

Approval Rate PAYMENTS / PROCESSING

  • Issuer decline rates (fraud, funds, limits)
  • Acquirer routing & processing
  • 3DS authentication result
  • Network token availability
  • Retry logic & cascade routing
  • Exemption strategy (TRA, low-value)
  • BIN-level performance differences
  • Currency & cross-border factors

3DS — Affects Both OVERLAP ZONE

  • 3DS challenge rate (high rate = more drop-off = lower CR)
  • 3DS completion rate (UX problem = CR impact)
  • 3DS provider performance (compare side-by-side)
  • Frictionless vs challenge ratio
  • 3DS version (v1 vs v2 performance)
  • Post-3DS approval rate (AR impact)

Key Insight for SDK PM YOUR LEVER

  • You own the gap between CR and AR
  • AR is mostly owned by payments/risk teams
  • Steps 1–4 are pure SDK territory
  • 3DS UX is shared ownership
  • A/B test SDK changes against CR, not AR
  • Track CR by SDK version to prove impact
